About

Hello! 👋 I'm Ji-Youn Jung, a Ph.D. student at the Human-Computer Interaction Institute at Carnegie Mellon University, advised by Prof. John Zimmerman.

My Research: Tools for Safer, More Successful AI Innovation

My research tackles a critical challenge: How can we innovate with AI responsibly and reduce the high failure rate of new AI ventures?

I focus on the crucial, early stages of design before any code is written. I build tangible tools and methods that embed responsible AI (RAI) principles directly into the creative process, helping teams maximize value while proactively minimizing harm. I use Generative AI not just as a subject of study, but as a core technology to build these new, enhanced design processes.

Current Project: GenAI for Technology-to-Market Matchmaking

My current project is a GenAI-powered system that automates technology-to-market matchmaking. This tool helps innovators with a novel technical capability—like a new algorithm or patent—systematically discover its most promising and safest market applications. It assesses thousands of possibilities against a unique framework of commercial, technical, ethical, and policy risks, empowering teams to build the right thing from the very beginning.

My Background

My path to HCI began with an interdisciplinary design education. I received my MSc in Design for Interaction from TU Delft and my BSc in Human-Environment and Design from Yonsei University, where I practiced product, graphic, and interaction design. My passion for understanding the 'why' behind user behavior led me to a minor in Cognitive Science, which forms the foundation of my human-centered approach to AI.

You can find a full list of my work on my Google Scholar and see my detailed experience in my CV. I share more frequent updates on Bluesky Social.
